Road Trip Scrapbook

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

Road Trip Scrapbook lets you pin your travel photos on a map and share the trip with family and friends. This policy explains what the app collects, how it’s used, and the control you have over it. In short: we collect only what the app needs to work, we don’t require an account, and we never sell your data or use advertising or tracking.

No account required

Road Trip Scrapbook does not ask you to create an account. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or a password. When you create a trip, the app generates private links (secret keys) that act as the “keys” to that trip. These keys are stored securely on your device and are the only way to access or edit a trip — anyone you share a link with can view the trip, so share carefully.

What we collect

We only collect the content you choose to add to a trip:

  • Trip details — the trip name and optional description you type.
  • Photos — the photos you choose to add, either by picking them from your library or capturing them with the camera.
  • Photo location & date — if a photo includes location and date information (from its embedded metadata, your device’s location when you take a photo, or a pin you drop manually), we use it to place the photo on the map.
  • Captions & place names — any captions you write and the place names shown for your photos.

Camera and photo access

The app asks for permission to use your camera and photo library so you can add photos to a trip. We only read the photos you specifically select or capture — the app never scans or uploads your photo library on its own.

Location

The app requests location access only to tag the photo you are taking with where it was taken. When granted, it takes a single location reading at the moment of capture — it does not track your location in the background or follow you over time. You can decline location access and still use the app fully; you can place photos on the map yourself by dropping a pin.

How your data is stored and used

The trips and photos you add are sent over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection and stored on Microsoft Azure cloud servers so they can be shown on the map and shared via your trip link. We use this information solely to provide the app’s features. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any unrelated purpose.

Third-party services

Road Trip Scrapbook keeps outside services to a minimum:

  • Microsoft Azure — hosts the app’s servers, database, and photo storage on our behalf.
  • Apple Maps (MapKit) — provides the map you see. The app uses Apple’s built-in maps, not Google or other third-party map providers.
  • OpenStreetMap (Nominatim) — to label a photo with a readable place name, the photo’s coordinates may be sent to OpenStreetMap’s geocoding service to look up the nearest place name.

The app contains no analytics, advertising, crash-tracking, or user-tracking software. We do not sell or rent your data to anyone.

Keeping and deleting your data

Your trips and photos are kept until you delete them. You are in control:

  • You can delete an individual photo at any time, which removes it from our servers.
  • You can permanently delete an entire trip, which removes the trip and all of its photos from our servers.

If you would like help deleting your data, contact us at the address below and we will take care of it.

Children’s privacy

Road Trip Scrapbook is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example, under the GDPR in Europe or the CCPA in California), you may have the right to access, correct, or delete your information, and to object to certain uses. Because the app works without an account, much of this is already in your hands — you can view and delete your trips and photos directly in the app. For any other request, contact us using the details below. We do not sell personal information.

Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS connections and stored on Microsoft Azure’s secured infrastructure. Trip keys are stored in your device’s secure Keychain. No method of transmission or storage is ever completely secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.

Contact us

Questions about this policy or your data? Email us at privacy@roadtripscrapbook.com. We aim to respond within 30 days.
