Route Line Styles

Current route is straight blue lines. Here are 5 alternatives.

1. Gentle Curves + Dash

Cubic bezier curves between points with a dashed stroke. Feels hand-drawn without being silly. Works at all distances.

2. Dotted Trail

Round dots instead of dashes, like breadcrumbs on a treasure map. Opacity fades for older segments. Playful but clean.

3. Wavy Road

A sinusoidal wobble applied to each segment. Amplitude scales with distance — short hops stay straight, long legs get wavier. Most "treasure map" feel.

4. Gradient Trail + Arrows

Solid curved line with a color gradient from start to finish (warm → cool). Small chevron arrows show direction. More polished, less whimsical.

5. Sketchy Hand-Drawn

Multiple thin offset strokes with slight randomness, like someone traced the route with a pen. Dashed. Most organic and personal feeling.

Current (for reference)

Straight blue lines, solid, 3px. No curves, no personality.
